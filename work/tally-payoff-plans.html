Tally Payoff Plans.

Senior Product Manager  ·  Tally

Found the line where user trust met business ambition.

// The Problem

Tally’s core product paid off users’ credit card debt. The challenge: designing payoff plans that got people to pay aggressively and sign up for autopay, without pushing so hard that we lost their trust or made them feel locked in.

The business wanted maximum payoff velocity. Users had a wide range of comfort levels around how aggressively they wanted to attack their debt. Designing for one meant failing the other. We needed to find the middle.

// The Solution

We ran 40 generative research sessions to understand how users actually thought about debt repayment, not how we assumed they did. The sessions surfaced a clear insight: users didn’t resist paying more, they resisted feeling out of control. Comfort level mattered more than dollar amount.

Armed with that, we went wide, exploring many different ways to visualize payoff plans and communicate progress. We tested gentle nudges rooted in user preference against a more aggressive ML-driven model built on spending data. The tension was real: user learnings pulled toward autonomy, business goals pulled toward acceleration.

We A/B tested across language, visualization, and plan structure until we landed on a winner: a payoff plan experience that gave users a sense of control while consistently nudging them toward faster payoff and autopay enrollment.
