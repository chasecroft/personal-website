WorkMade Onboarding.

Head of Product  ·  WorkMade

Replaced a 20-minute slog with a smart default.

// The Problem

WorkMade’s onboarding had a compounding set of problems that were killing activation:

  • Users didn’t understand what qualified as a tax write-off and churned out of confusion
  • Onboarding required manually sorting through hundreds of transactions — a process taking upwards of 20 minutes to complete
  • Using a debit card for non-write-off purchases was flagging potential audit risk for consumers, adding anxiety to an already confusing experience

The result was an onboarding funnel that was hemorrhaging users before they ever saw the product’s value.

// The Solution

We started with research — conducting sessions with users to map the mental models they actually held around tax write-offs and audit risk. Most of their anxiety came from uncertainty, not complexity. The product was asking them to make decisions they didn’t feel equipped to make.

The fix was to stop asking. We built and tested prototypes that used AI to do the classification work for the user — leveraging spending patterns, occupation, location, and dozens of other data points to intelligently predict whether a given expense was a write-off or not.

The initial model launched at 92% accuracy. With more usage data flowing in, the model only improves over time.
