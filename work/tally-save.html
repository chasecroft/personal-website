Tally Save.

Senior Product Manager  ·  Tally

Pulled a product off the chopping block.

// The Problem

Tally was a line of credit product with a very high CAC, north of $1,000. The plan was to launch a savings and wealth-building app as a front door: lower acquisition cost, gather data, and upsell into the core product.

I launched Tally Save to market. It had 80% month-over-month retention, which was strong. But CAC came in at 2x what we needed. The product was on the chopping block.

// The Solution

I ran a team of 40 people across design, engineering, and marketing to audit the full cohesion of our ads, app store copy, and onboarding. We held daily war rooms to pressure-test ideas and make sure everything worked together as a single story, not a collection of disconnected campaigns.

We ran extensive research and A/B tested across every layer of the funnel. Six weeks and 25 coordinated changes later, we went back to market with a fundamentally stronger narrative from top to bottom.

What we implemented:

  • Social proof woven throughout the acquisition flow
  • More manageable goal-setting to reduce early drop-off
  • Reward systems to gamify signup and early deposits
  • Location-based bank selection to reduce friction
  • Streaks to encourage recurring deposits and build habit
  • Personalization across onboarding to match user context
